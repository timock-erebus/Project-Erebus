The man hated ferries.

He hated the smell of diesel, the wobble underfoot, and the kind of people who enjoyed talking on boats — loud, sun-wrinkled people with hiking boots and time to kill. He stood at the bow, wrapped in a waxed jacket that hadn’t been waterproofed in years, sipping from a paper coffee cup that smelled faintly of bleach and contained mostly cheap whiskey he’d acquired whilst the captain wasn’t watching. It tasted like someone had wrung out a floor mop into it.

He drank it anyway.
